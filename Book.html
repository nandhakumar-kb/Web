Ikigai

by Héctor García & Francesc Miralles

Buy Now

Summary

"Ikigai: The Japanese Secret to a Long and Happy Life" explores the Japanese concept of "ikigai," which translates to "a reason for being." The book takes readers to Okinawa, Japan, one of the world's Blue Zones where people live exceptionally long lives.

Through interviews with residents, the authors uncover the secrets to their longevity and happiness. They reveal how finding one's ikigai—the intersection of what you love, what you are good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for—is fundamental to a fulfilling life. The book provides practical tools to help readers discover their own ikigai and live with more purpose.

The Concept of Ikigai

The Ikigai model helps visualize the four overlapping elements that lead to a sense of purpose.

Why I Love This Book

I love "Ikigai" for its simple yet profound wisdom. It's a calming and inspiring read that shifts your perspective on life, work, and happiness. The book doesn't just present a philosophy; it offers actionable advice on how to incorporate small, meaningful changes into your daily routine. It's a beautiful reminder that the secret to a happy life isn't about grand achievements, but about finding joy and purpose in the everyday.
